Cash & Cash Equivalents

If you want to make a positive impact with your liquid assets, you essentially have two questions to ask yourself . . .

1)

Do you want to move a portion of your liquid assets into high-impact deposits and CDs?

You can earn competitive CD-rate returns with FDIC insurance for virtually any size deposit - even very large deposits, all while supporting community development banks.

  Learn More  

Here's how it works:

  1. 1) Select a Community Development Bank in a location (or multiple locations) where you'd like to make an impact - see map below
  2. 2) Make a deposit to that bank and they'll offer you competitive CD rates
  3. 3) You'll get FDIC coverage on up to $30 Million (instead of the normal $250K) via the CDARS system
  4. 4) The Community Development Bank you select will get to use 100% of the money you deposited to do their good work in the community

This unique opportunity is made possible by the Banking on Communities program, which is a collaboration between the Community Development Banking Association (CDBA) and the CDARS financial network.

+

=

  Learn more about Community Development Banks!

Community Development Banks make a significant difference in the lives of tens of thousands of people across the country. Community Development Banking Association (CDBA) members deliver sustainable impact through innovative financial products and services for low and moderate income communities that are left out of the economic mainstream. Community Development Banks are often the only source of credit and financial services in these underserved communities. They make loans to build and renovate housing so that people have a decent place to live. Their housing lending, in turn, sparks revitalization of other housing in local neighborhoods. They make loans to small businesses so that people will have jobs. The businesses they lend to, in turn, act as magnets that draw other businesses into the community. Community Development Banks' lending has a ripple effect throughout the community far beyond their direct customers, changing a communitys dynamic.

Click here to be taken to the CDBA website for more info . . .

Click on any location marker for details - contact the designated bank representative for info about making a deposit that supports the banks' local community development efforts . . .
